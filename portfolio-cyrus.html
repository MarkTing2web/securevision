The Challenge

High-Value Site Undermined by Constant False Alarms

Cyrus Tech's manufacturing facility stores high-value components and IP-sensitive equipment. The existing perimeter detection system — motion-activated lights and basic PIR sensors — generated constant false alarms from wildlife, weather events, and vehicle headlights. Guards were responding to non-events nightly, leading to alarm fatigue and genuine security gaps.

  • PIR sensors generated false alarms from cats, birds, and vehicle lights
  • Guards desensitised to alarms — genuine threats risked being ignored
  • No biometric verification for restricted production zones — shared PIN codes
  • Manual contractor logbook — no reliable vehicle movement audit trail
  • Perimeter had coverage gaps between sensor zones in darkness
  • No integration between perimeter alerts and camera evidence
The Solution

AI-Powered Perimeter + Biometric Zone Control

Securevision replaced the entire perimeter detection architecture with a thermal + visible camera combination on all fence lines — eliminating false positives through AI classification. Biometric access was deployed at all restricted zones, and ANPR fleet management replaced the manual logbook.

  • Thermal cameras on all perimeter fence lines — heat-signature detection in total darkness
  • AI classification distinguishes humans from animals and vehicles — eliminates false alarms
  • PTZ auto-tracking cameras engage automatically on AI-confirmed intrusion
  • Biometric fingerprint terminals at all production zone entry points
  • ANPR system manages all contractor and fleet vehicle entry/exit
  • Unified alert dashboard — perimeter breach triggers camera + security team notification
